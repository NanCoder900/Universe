The starting of a new world of Science in India.

The launch of the first Indian rocket fron Thumba near Thiruvanathapuram, Kerala on 21 November 1963, marked the beginning of the Indian Space Programme. ISRO started launching indigenously made sounding rockets from 1965 and experience gained was of immense value in the mastering of solid proppelant technology. In 1975, all sounding rocket activities were consolidated under the Rohini Sounding Rocket (RSR) Programme.

Success comes when you really want something, no matter what.

Many of you have heard about the "Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM)". It was India's first interplanetary mission and it made it the fourth space agency to achieve Mars orbit, after Roscosmos, NASA, and the European Space Agency. It is also known as "Mangalyaan". If you research more about it then you will get to know that the cost of the whole mission was something around ₹450 Crore, which is less than the budget of a Hollywood film! So you can see anything is possible if you really want it to be possible.

Why does the moon change its shape?

The moon which causes high tides amd low tides on the Earth is a sphere. Actually, it doesn't change it's shape; only our view of it changes. The reason is that unlike stars, the moon does not produce light, but only refects the light from the Sun. Since the moon goes around the Earth once in a month, the orientation of the moon, the sun, the Earth respective to one another changes. For this reason , the illuminated part of the moon cannot always be seen clearly from all the Earth. Sometimes we see it full, sometimes half, and sometimes not at all. But the side of the moon that faces us remains the same as the crates and the dark spots called the "mares" are always visible along the moon.
